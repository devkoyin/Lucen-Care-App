A — Role Selector In The Hero

User pathways are front-and-centre. Zero ambiguity about who the platform serves. Fast routing — users click their role directly from the hero. Risk: the grid of four competes with the headline for attention on first load.

B — Split Hero, Role Detail Below

Strong editorial hero with a single focused CTA ("Find My Path"). Role detail cards appear as the user scrolls — narrative builds before asking them to choose. More premium feel, better for first impressions and presentations.

C — Full-Bleed Dark Hero, Floating Role Strip

Bold, tech-forward, app-like. Single powerful CTA above the fold, role strip as a visual anchor below. Very strong on a presentation screen. Risk: "Get Started" without role context may confuse first-time visitors who don't know which path is theirs.

My recommendation: B. The split hero earns attention before asking users to choose — and it photographs well in demos. But A is faster for return users who already know their role. Click your preference or tell me what to adjust.
